# Scriptures Text Library

### Hebrew Bible

* **The Hebrew Bible (Tanakh):**  *(Hebrew)* This canonical collection of Hebrew texts comprises the Torah, Nevi'im (Prophets), and Ketuvim (Writings), based on the accurate Leningrad Codex. https://www.sefaria.org.il/download/version/Genesis%20-%20he%20-%20Tanach%20with%20Ta'amei%20Hamikra.json

#### Targums (Aramaic Translations of the Hebrew Bible)

* **Targum Onkelos (Torah):**  *(Babylonian Aramaic)* A translation of the Torah (Pentateuch), considered the oldest and most widely used. It likely originated in Palestine but was transmitted and edited in Babylonia.
* **Targum Jonathan (Nevi'im):** *(Babylonian Aramaic)* A translation of the Prophets (Nevi'im), possibly originating in Palestine.
* **Targum Pseudo-Jonathan (Torah):** *(Palestinian Aramaic)* A translation of the Torah, traditionally attributed to Jonathan ben Uzziel.
* **Fragment Targums (Various Books):** *(Various Aramaic dialects)* Incomplete versions found in various sources, including the Cairo Geniza.
* **Targums to the Writings (Ketuvim):** *(Late Jewish Literary Aramaic)* Translations of various books in the Ketuvim (Writings), including Job, Psalms, Chronicles, and the Five Megilloth (Ruth, Esther, Song of Songs, Lamentations, Ecclesiastes), and Proverbs. Targums for Ezra, Nehemiah, and Daniel are unknown.

#### Commentaries

* **Rashi's Commentary on the Torah:** *(Hebrew)* Written by Rabbi Shlomo Yitzchaki (Rashi) in the 11th century, this commentary provides insights into the Torah text.

#### Additional Texts

* **JPS 1917 English Translation of the Hebrew Bible:** *(English)* Published by the Jewish Publication Society in 1917, this translation is known for its accuracy and fidelity to the original Hebrew. https://www.sefaria.org.il/download/version/Genesis%20-%20en%20-%20The%20Holy%20Scriptures:%20A%20New%20Translation%20(JPS%201917).json

* **Saadia Gaon's Tafsir:** *(Arabic)* Created in the 10th century, this Arabic translation and commentary on the Hebrew Bible aimed to make biblical texts accessible to Arabic-speaking Jews.

### Mishnah and Talmuds

* **Mishnah:** *(Hebrew)* The Mishnah is a foundational compilation of Hebrew oral law, codified into six orders.
* **Babylonian Talmud:** *(Babylonian Aramaic)* An extensive compendium of Rabbinic discourse on the Mishnah.
* **Jerusalem Talmud (Talmud Yerushalmi):** *(Palestinian Aramaic)* This text elaborates on the Mishnah with unique insights.

#### Commentaries

* **Ovadia of Bartenura's Commentary on the Mishnah:** *(Hebrew)* Authored by Rabbi Ovadia Bartenura in the 16th century, this commentary offers analysis of the Mishnah.
* **Rashi's Commentary on the Mishnah:** *(Hebrew)* Written by Rabbi Shlomo Yitzchaki (Rashi) in the 11th century, this commentary elucidates the Mishnah's teachings.

### Zohar

* **The Zohar:** *(mix of Palestinian Aramaic and Babylonian Aramaic)* A key mystical text of Kabbalah.

#### Additional texts

* **New Zohar:** *(mix of Palestinian Aramaic and Babylonian Aramaic)* An addition to the original Zohar.
* **Tikkuney haZohar:** *(mix of Palestinian Aramaic and Babylonian Aramaic)* A collection of mystical teachings and prayers associated with the Zohar.
