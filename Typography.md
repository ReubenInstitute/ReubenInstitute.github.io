## Wide Letter Forms

Wide letter forms are employed in specific phonetic contexts:

- The divine name (יהוה) uses wide He letters (ﬣ). [יﬣוﬣ]
- For *Ayin* (ע) and *Het* (ח) with a "late" patah vowel (patach ganuv). רוּחַ שָׁבוּעַ
- For final *He* (ה) with mapik (dagesh indicating consonantal pronunciation). סִפְרָהּ
- Midword for *Aleph* (א), *Ayin* (ע), *He* (ה), and *Het* (ח) when carrying sheva. יַמַחְשֵׁב מַעְבָּדָה נֶהְפָּךְ
## Font Styling

- Verse text appears in a larger, dark serif font for emphasis and readability.
- Verse numbers and other secondary elements use an understated light gray sans-serif.
- Titles use muted-orange sans-serif.
- Descriptions use light-gray sans-serif.
