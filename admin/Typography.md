# Typography

## Diacritic Rendering

Dagesh hazak and sheva na are displayed distinctly from regular dagesh and sheva—rendered as slightly larger squares instead of circles—ensuring they immediately stand out visually.

## Wide Letter Forms

Wide letter forms are employed in specific phonetic contexts:
- The divine name (יהוה) uses wide He letters (ﬣ).
- For *Ayin* (ע) and *Het* (ח) with a "late" patah vowel (patach ganuv).
- For final *He* (ה) with mapik (dagesh indicating consonantal pronunciation).
- Midword for *Aleph* (א), *Ayin* (ע), *He* (ה), and *Het* (ח) when carrying sheva.

## Font Styling

- Verse text appears in a larger, dark serif font for emphasis and readability.
- Verse numbers and other secondary elements use an understated light gray sans-serif.
- Titles use muted-orange sans-serif.
- Descriptions use light-gray sans-serif.
