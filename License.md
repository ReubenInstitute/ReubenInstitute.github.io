## Scriptures text

The biblical text comes from the Leningrad Codex, an 11th‑century manuscript that's firmly in the public domain. The modern punctuation, design choices, and formatting added by the Institute are also shared freely, without any claim of ownership. Anyone can use, copy, change, or share the text however they like.

## Original audio (1970)

The verse recordings are taken from an old reading made around 1970. In most places, that's old enough for the copyright to have run out, so the audio is pretty much free to use. Copyright rules are different in different countries, though, and if someone somewhere still has a valid claim, that's between them and the person using the files. The Institute provides these recordings as a piece of history—the Institute didn't make the recording, doesn't claim to own it, and can't give any official advice about its legal standing.

## Cloned audio

These voice tracks were made by running the original verse recordings through ElevenLabs voice cloning. The starting material is pretty much in the public domain, and the Institute doesn't claim any ownership over the resulting files. If there are any leftover rights tied to the original recording, the cloning process, or the platform's rules, that's for the people involved to sort out—the Institute just provides the files as they are, without claiming to own them or playing lawyer about it.

## Multimedia videos

The video series (Parashah, Psalms, Piyutim) are put together from several pieces: images and video made by Midjourney, music and soundscapes made by Suno, voice tracks cloned by ElevenLabs, and text taken from public sources. The Institute doesn't claim any ownership over the finished videos. They can be watched, shared, or downloaded freely. The only thing asked is that no one passes them off as their own original work. Each piece comes with its own rules from the platform that made it, and getting to grips with those rules is between the user and those platforms.

## Piyutim music

The musical tracks are songs made by Suno. The Institute doesn't claim any ownership over them. The only condition is that they shouldn't be shared as if they were your own original work. Apart from that, there are no limits on what can be done with them.
