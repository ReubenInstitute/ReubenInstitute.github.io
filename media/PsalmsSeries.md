# Psalms Series

The **Psalms Series** publishes individual chapters of the *Book of Psalms*.

Each chapter is set to a background song generated with **Suno**, accompanied by video segments created with **Midjourney**. A separate raw version (without music or background imagery) is also published.

**Audio foundation:** The narration is based on Abraham Shmuelov’s historic Leningrad Codex readings, refined with AI voice cloning for clarity while retaining the original Sephardic pronunciation.

**Distribution:** YouTube, YouTube Shorts, Instagram, TikTok, Spotify (audio-only), WhatsApp Channel, and the IVR phone line.

### Psalm 51

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1bsmN82HUHPGS8bKB2Q5MQ6ZcWMk3i_ps/view"><img src="img/psalm-051.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 51"></a> David's profound and transparent prayer of repentance, composed after being confronted for his sin with Bathsheba. It moves beyond a simple plea for mercy to a deep longing for internal transformation — famously seeking a clean heart and a steadfast spirit. David acknowledges that true restoration comes not through ritual or sacrifice, but through a broken and contrite heart. The psalm concludes with a prayer for the spiritual and physical rebuilding of Zion.

### Psalm 53

### Psalm 54

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1ouTWy8kd30oJR0ixrZ-Z5ezAcbCzUz96/view"><img src="img/psalm-054.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 54"></a> An urgent plea for rescue by David, composed when the Ziphites betrayed his hiding place to Saul. The mood shifts from a cry for help to a confident declaration of trust, acknowledging God as the sustainer of his life, and closes with a vow of a freewill offering and praise for deliverance from every trouble.

### Psalm 56

The psalm pivots on a declaration of trust: even in fear, David chooses confidence in divine protection. He contrasts the fleeting schemes of mortals with the eternal reliability of the Divine's word, ending with a vow of thanksgiving and a firm belief that he will yet walk in the light of life.

### Psalm 59

The psalm moves from the dread of evening to a morning song of strength, celebrating the Divine as an unshakeable fortress.

### Psalm 60

David recalls ancient promises of territorial victory, moving from the despair of defeat to a bold plea for help, and concludes with the conviction that human aid is fleeting — but through divine strength, the people will prevail.

### Psalm 90

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1tI_Z8d2rgQ7ClxnJ70qHPC2GZJ7ouQ05/view"><img src="img/psalm-090.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 90"></a> A Prayer of Moses reflecting on the vast scale of eternity against the fleeting nature of human life. It opens by grounding the soul in the Divine as an eternal dwelling place — existing before the mountains were formed — and contrasts this with the frailty of mortals who vanish like grass or a dream. Moses asks for the wisdom to number our days and for morning to bring a renewal of mercy, ending with a poignant plea for the Divine to establish the work of our hands so that our brief lives might leave a lasting mark.

### Psalm 99

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1ACdy7lH45ukodDIB7sZJiqo3LIA2VDrX/view"><img src="img/psalm-099.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 99"></a> A majestic proclamation of the LORD's holy kingship. ### Psalm 100

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1ubu_6waZ5bAzZHiPOK2iN-k5M6yulL2s/view"><img src="img/psalm-100.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 100"></a> A vibrant, universal call to gratitude and worship. Known as the Psalm of Thanksgiving, it invites all the earth to enter God's presence with joyful singing and shouts of praise. The psalm grounds this celebration in two fundamental truths: our identity as God's people — the sheep of His pasture — and the unchanging nature of His character. Moving from the outer gates of the sanctuary to the inner courts of the heart, it declares that His goodness is total, His mercy is everlasting, and His faithfulness spans every generation.

### Psalm 101

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1gox4WxF2-Ubg95PkO2KJExQtIyO20c7e/view"><img src="img/psalm-101.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 101"></a> A solemn vow of leadership by David, outlining the ethical standards he sets for his own life and royal court. It serves as a personal and political manifesto of integrity, in which the psalmist commits to walking with a blameless heart and refusing to tolerate deceit, slander, or arrogance in his presence. David pledges to seek out the faithful as companions and to daily root out wickedness from the city of the LORD, ensuring that justice and truth define his reign.

### Psalm 117

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1bXQo3Cm-LSkKibk7qAynFsEI0eEZ8pIg/view"><img src="img/psalm-117.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 117"></a> The shortest chapter in the entire Bible, yet its message is expansive. It is a universal call for all nations and peoples to join in praise of the Creator, built on two pillars of the divine character: steadfast love and eternal faithfulness. Brief as it is, the psalm serves as a reminder that the relationship between the Divine and humanity is built on truths that transcend every border and every generation.

### Psalm 129

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1fRYvngnwinEUMAm9hCGdBCL8gottb31A/view"><img src="img/psalm-129.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 129"></a> A communal song of resilience reflecting on a long history of national suffering and survival. It employs the vivid image of plowers scarring a back with long furrows to describe past oppressions, yet celebrates the fact that no adversary has ever truly prevailed. The psalm calls for the sudden withering of those who hate Zion — likened to rooftop grass that dries up before it can grow — and closes by withholding from them even the customary blessing of passersby.

### Psalm 130

<a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1s-ZEz7Pvp2Bx16HBj2Nzo9BpaeEkLP6k/view"><img src="img/psalm-130.png" width="120" alt="Psalm 130"></a> A profound song of ascent journeying from the lightless depths of despair to the certain hope of dawn. It begins with a raw cry for mercy, acknowledging that if God kept a strict record of sins, no human could stand. The heart of the psalm is the transformative power of forgiveness, which fosters true reverence. The psalmist describes a state of intense, patient waiting — more eager than watchmen longing for morning — and closes with a communal call for Israel to hope in the LORD, whose redemption is as plentiful as His love is steadfast.
