# 5 Jan 2026 – DeepSeek 3 – Aramaic course development

_Aramaic_

A subscription‑based Aramaic language course was designed from the ground up, combining rigorous Eastern Aramaic scholarship with a minimalist visual pedagogy.

### Curriculum

The 6‑class progression moves from Nouns through Adjectives, Numbers, Pronouns, and Verbs to Prepositions & Particles.  Each class is built around a dense dialogue table that links Hebrew script, IPA transcription, and physical actions — grammar taught through demonstration, not explanation.

### Figurine‑based instruction

The visual world is populated by plasteline figurines with gender‑differentiated silhouettes (hair and skirt for feminine), facial features that appear only when needed (mouth for speaking, nose for smelling, ears for hearing), and roles indicated by minimal props — a crown, a hammer, a crook.  Everything looks mouldable, slightly chubby, agile.  The professor appears as an arm with a pointing stick against a green chalkboard; the figurine world unfolds on a sterile blank floor with soft geometry and muted textures.

### Teaching methodology

Spatial logic and pointing drive the instruction.  Gender reinforcement is built in: male figurines point to feminine objects, female figurines to masculine ones.  Lessons progress from isolated vocabulary to sequential phrases to applied conversation.  Class 3 (Numbers 1‑3) and Class 4 (Pronouns — a 32‑row table with full pointing logic) were completed as production‑ready storyboards.

The course is pedagogically sound, visually defined, and consistent across platforms — designed for TikTok and Instagram but scalable to full courses.  Next step: a pilot video to validate visual execution before scaling production.
