# Jul 2025 – Gemini 2.5 – Strategic following and engagement plan

_Social_

Not all followers carry equal weight.  We drew a sharp line between the passive consumer who follows 3 000 channels and the “powerful user” whose own significant following can amplify the Institute’s signal.  Engagement from powerful users – likes, comments, shares – tells the algorithm to pay attention and exposes the content to a wider audience.

That insight shaped a two‑part public‑curation and engagement strategy.

### Part A: Strategic following as public curation

The “Following” list isn’t a personal feed; it’s a shop window.  Six accounts were chosen to define the brand by association:
* **האקדמיה ללשון העברית** – the ultimate authority in the core niche, lending instant credibility.
* **Arum Natzorkhang** – a powerful user in adjacent scholarly fields (ancient languages), placing the Institute inside a wider academic world.
* **Babbel** – connecting the brand to a mainstream, language‑education audience.
* **www.PersianPoetics.com** – linking to a parallel audience in linguistic and cultural history.
* **אברהם רבינוביץ** – an engaging, non‑dogmatic approach to Judaism that appeals to a broader, non‑religious crowd.
* **ירעם נתניהו** – a key local expert and language advisor, positioning the Institute as a peer in the Israeli professional community.

### Part B: Strategic engagement for proactive growth

Engagement must be a high‑quality contribution, not a cheap promotion:
* Craft **expert comments** – insightful, value‑adding, and free of sales‑pitch energy.
* Use **Duet or Stitch** to respectfully add a unique perspective (a historical audio clip, a linguistic insight).
* The ultimate goal is **reciprocity**: consistent, valuable presence that leads those creators to notice, engage, and eventually share the Institute’s work.

Together, the two parts build an authoritative brand that is both a trusted curator (the “Following” list) and an active, respected peer in the community – growing not through trends, but through genuine scholarly value.
