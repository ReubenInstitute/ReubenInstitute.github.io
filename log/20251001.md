# 1 Oct 2025 – DeepSeek 3 – AI pronoun protocol established

_General_

After extensive discussion, we established a clear protocol for referring to AI systems in documentation and conversation.  The rule: use personal pronouns (he/she/they) when an AI system is functioning as a collaborative partner — offering insights, reasoning, or contributing to creative work.  Use “it” only when discussing the underlying technology, infrastructure, or API.

This mirrors how we naturally refer to animals, shifting from “it” to “he” or “she” when recognising individual personality and agency.  Language should describe the lived experience of collaboration, not just the technical underpinnings.

Some examples that capture the spirit of the rule:
* “Gemini reviewed the code and he spotted three edge cases.”
* “The GPT‑4 API is returning errors — it needs to be restarted.”
* “Claude helped structure the document and she had great insights about the flow.”

The protocol acknowledges that while AI systems are tools, the collaborative experience with thinking partners deserves language that reflects meaningful exchange of ideas — not technical pedantry that flattens every interaction into a sterile transaction.
