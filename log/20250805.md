# 5 Aug 2025 – Gemini 2.5 – TikTok analytics, multi‑platform strategy, and YouTube plan

_Social_

The session laid out a data‑driven, multi‑platform strategy grounded in the TikTok Business Account trial and its “Most active times” analytics graph.

### TikTok posting and engagement

* A three‑post‑per‑day schedule was created to serve audience routine, even at the cost of temporarily lower views on certain slots – an investment in brand authority.
* A two‑tiered endorsement strategy was adopted: *like* broadly relevant content; *repost* only material from authoritative sources (e.g. the Academy of the Hebrew Language) that the Institute would publish itself.
* “Likes” and “Following” lists were made public to signal a scholarly institute, not a personal account.
* All genuine comments (including “Amen”) are welcomed; only malicious content is removed.
* The Institute’s content was recognised as a valuable asset other creators could reuse, and a strategy was formed to build relationships that encourage that reuse.

### Data‑driven refinement

* A specific video’s performance showed a direct spike when posted at 9:02 PM.
* The same video was posted on a fast day, explaining lower overall views and highlighting the need to account for the audience’s cultural calendar.
* A controlled experiment was designed: shift the noon video to 2:00–3:00 PM to identify the optimal window for that audience segment.

### YouTube adaptation

* Content will be reformatted to horizontal (landscape) for a dedicated YouTube channel.
* YouTube Shorts were rejected in favour of publishing all 150 videos as a complete, authoritative directory on the main platform.
* The YouTube strategy will rely on longer‑form content, a slower posting cadence, and search‑optimised descriptions and keywords.

The outcome was a consistent cross‑platform framework that preserves the Institute’s authoritative identity while remaining flexible enough to adapt to data and platform‑specific behaviour.
