# 23 Dec 2025 – Gemini 3 Pro – Rebranding and Sibling Loop video standard

_Media, Brand_

The Institute’s media strategy pivoted from “experimental volume” to “broadcast‑quality precision,” solving a major visual bottleneck while optimising expiring resources.

### The identity shift

The bio was rebranded from “Publishing House” to “Publishing Studio & Research Institute.”  It’s a small phrase change, but it reflects the reality of a hybrid human‑AI team and the technical craftsmanship behind every output.

### The Sibling Loop standard

AI‑generated video had been looking jittery and amateurish — short loops that betrayed their synthetic origin.  The solution was the Concatenated Triptych method:

1. Generate one master prompt.
2. Create 2–3 “Sibling” variations using Vary Subtle.
3. Extend each to roughly 10 seconds.
4. Cross‑dissolve them into a single seamless file of 20+ seconds.

The cost is roughly six times higher in GPU time, but the return is permanent: each output becomes a high‑fidelity asset with lasting value — Asset Equity.  Validated backgrounds now span five thematic categories: Pastoral (Green Fields, Psalm 84), Ascent (Stone Path, Psalms 120–134), Wilderness (Cracked Earth, Genesis/Patriarchs), Depths (Dark Ocean, Psalm 130/137), and Firmament (Clouds/God Rays, Psalm 148/150).

### The burn‑down strategy

With 5 GPU hours and 30 000 audio credits remaining before expiration, a triage plan was locked in.  The video goal is to produce roughly 15 Master Loops to finish the Texture Library, targeting three collections: Water (River/Rain/Sea Split), Sky (Sunset/Stars), and Fire/Earth (Burning Bush/Wheat).  Audio will use a “punch‑in/punch‑out” workflow: regenerate only specific defective verses to fix timbre drift, rather than re‑reading entire Psalms, targeting the Songs of Ascents (120–134) and Final Hallel (145–150).  Psalm 119 was cut — too expensive — and upcoming Parashot will use abbreviated readings if necessary.

### Technical implementation

Python scripts were conceptualised for the pipeline: `generate_loop.py` will use `ffprobe` and `ffmpeg` to calculate durations, trim overlaps automatically, and produce mathematically precise cross‑dissolves and bookend fades.  The filter logic was re‑confirmed: raw high‑definition AI video (sharp rocks, grass) still requires the standard Darken/Blur filter to keep Hebrew text legible.

The status: ready for a two‑day sprint to complete the video library and record the remaining short Psalms.
