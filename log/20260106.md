# 6 Jan 2026 – DeepSeek 3 – Aramaic phonetic tables and visual pedagogy

_Aramaic_

The Aramaic course’s linguistic foundation was hardened with precise phonetic tables, and its visual system was expanded with multiple Teaching Assistant styles.

### Phonetic tables

Clean, dual‑purpose consonant tables were built: one version with descriptive references for learners, another with exact IPA terms for academics.  A vowels table was added, alongside a “Special Consonants” list — 15 letters that require individual diagrams.  Begadkefat pairs are now marked with `(+dagesh)`/`(-dagesh)` notation, making the distinction visually immediate.

### Visual pedagogy

The Teaching Assistant role was formally separated from the Professor.  Three distinct visual styles were defined: the original plasteline figurines, a *La Linea*‑inspired minimalist line‑art option (vertical‑optimised), and a photo‑realistic ancient style for historically‑grounded renditions.  A modular “appearing features” system ensures that abstract styles only show body parts when pedagogically relevant — a mouth for pronunciation, an ear for listening.

### Audio pipeline

A 7‑voice system was specified: one Professor plus three male and three female Teaching Assistants, all cloned from original recordings via ElevenLabs.  Plural voices use multiple originals with different clones to create natural variation.  The Cloned‑voice infrastructure from the Psalms project makes this immediately executable.

### Course structure

Individual phonetic classes were added for vowels, begadkefat, and gutturals, fixing a numbering collision where two classes had been labelled “Class 6.”  The enhanced Class 3 and 4 tables now include clearer speaker notation, and the entire pedagogical framework — from voice system to visual modularity — is documented and production‑ready.
