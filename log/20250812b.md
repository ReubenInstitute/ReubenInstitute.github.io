# 12 Aug 2025 – Gemini 2.5 – Two‑pillar content and subscription plan

_Business_

The monetisation strategy was refined into a concrete two‑pillar plan, directly addressing the three core concerns that had surfaced.

### The core problems solved

* **“Why subscribe?”** – early access alone is weak motivation.  The solution: a unique product (multi‑language translations) that is never released publicly, making the subscription the only way to get it.
* **“Too pretentious?”** – launching a subscription with zero subscribers feels awkward.  The solution: a phased launch that uses free content as proof of concept, building a proven audience before asking anyone to pay.
* **The audience is asking** – a large portion of the new Latin‑American followers were explicitly requesting translations, confirming that translation was both a public‑growth tool and a subscription value driver.

### Pillar 1: Multi‑Language Psalms videos

This is the primary driver of public growth and the accessible entry point to the subscription.
* **Initial public launch** – for the first 10‑15 videos, one new multi‑language Psalm will be released each week to the public.  Each is a “hybrid” featuring the original Hebrew content with subtitles in Spanish, English, and Modern Hebrew.  This directly answers audience demand and acts as the most effective public advertisement.
* **Ongoing subscription split** – after the initial launch, every new Psalm is produced in two versions:
  * *Public version* – a Hebrew‑only video released weekly to attract a wide audience.
  * *Subscriber version* – a video with the multi‑language translations, released exclusively to subscribers and never made public.

### Pillar 2: Aramaic video series

This is the core, long‑term value proposition that establishes scholarly authority and justifies the subscription cost.
* **Limited public showcase** – a small, curated set of Aramaic videos (around 10‑15) will be released publicly.  They showcase the unique, painstakingly reconstructed pronunciation, serving as a permanent gallery that proves the value of the work.
* **Exclusive library** – the vast majority of Aramaic content is produced solely for subscribers.  This ever‑growing, exclusive library is the main reason to subscribe – a permanent benefit unavailable anywhere else.

### Technical and financial decisions

* **Technical feasibility** – the existing video production app can easily generate a second video type with subtitles, making the entire strategy a minor, scalable workflow adjustment.
* **Pricing** – a single tier at **$9.99 / month**.  TikTok’s one‑tier subscription model makes a single price point the natural choice, and it grants access to all benefits of both pillars.
* **Launch plan** – the first 10‑15 hybrid videos will be released publicly to prove value and build a solid audience.  The exact subscription launch timing will be dictated by the audience’s response.

The plan turned the Psalms Project into a two‑pillar content engine: one for growth, one for authority, both feeding a single sustainable subscription.
