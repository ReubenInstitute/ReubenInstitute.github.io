# 14 Dec 2025 – DeepSeek 3 – Video production pipeline and TikTok revival

_Media, Social_

A single Psalm proved the entire pipeline still worked, and it revived a dying channel in the process.

### Background asset pipeline

We refined the Midjourney prompt template to enforce our now‑standardised visual language: `[scene description], photorealistic, cinematic, atmospheric, serene, shallow depth of field, muted palette`.  Several visual styles for background videos were written down for the first time.  An FFmpeg post‑processing pipeline was established to give every clip our signature washed‑out look, and we tested across platforms — Midjourney as the primary generator, Google AI (nanobanana/Veo3) as a secondary for evaluation.

### Psalm 70 as production proof

We put Psalm 70 through the full system: a custom Midjourney background (a washed‑out lake scene), Suno background music mixed at 20% volume, the AI‑cloned Hebrew narration, and professional text overlays with correct timing.  The `BaseVideo` class was updated with three features that made the output feel finished: background music auto‑mixing from `{psalm_number:03d}.mp3`, global 0.5‑second fades on both video and audio, and stereo output support.

### TikTok revival

The channel had been stagnant — over 5 000 followers with a net‑negative growth rate.  Psalm 70 changed that in 14 hours: 1 973 views, 266 likes, 30 saves, 22 shares.  Average watch time increased from 19.1 seconds to 20.4 seconds.  Three new followers arrived, and the revival began.

### What we learned

The content formula was validated: washed backgrounds plus music plus the cloned voice equals engagement.  The target audience is niche — the 40% who watch to the end are exactly the right audience.  Saves and shares indicate real‑world use for prayer and reflection, not passive scrolling.  And consistency beats virality: daily posting with quality, not chasing algorithmic spikes, is the sustainable path forward.
