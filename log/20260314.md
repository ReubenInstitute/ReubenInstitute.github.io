# 14 Mar 2026 – DeepSeek 3 – Psalms audio library completely rebuilt, multi‑format export

_Media, PsalmSeries_

We wiped the slate and rebuilt the entire Psalms audio library from raw cloned verses to finished multi‑format releases.  Every verse that had exhibited timbre drift or clipping was regenerated.  Word‑level alignment was backfilled for all 150 psalms — thousands of verses — and the text was standardised: curly braces, tildes, asterisks, and zero‑width joiners were stripped while legitimate punctuation and Jerusalem spelling variants were preserved.

The ElevenLabs Dark Knox 2 voice generated 150 individual psalm titles, five book titles (“ספר ראשון” through “ספר חמישי”), 28 daily titles for the four‑week balanced reading method, and the master “תהילים” title.  New audio classes were built to assemble these into compilations: `PsalmAudio` gained the psalm title as its first segment with silence fades; `PsalmsBookAudio` compiled each book with its book title followed by all its psalms; `DailyPsalmsAudio` assembled the 28 daily portions; `PsalmsCompleteAudio` concatenated the five book MP3s behind the main title; `FourWeekCompleteAudio` did the same for the daily cycle.

The export run produced 150 individual psalm MP3s, five book MP3s, 28 daily MP3s, a sequential complete edition at 5 h 39 min (156 MB), and a four‑week complete edition at 5 h 37 min (155 MB).  When ffmpeg threw an “Argument list too long” error on the full concatenation command, we switched to pre‑exported intermediate files, which eliminated the shell limit without touching the audio pipeline.  ID3 tagging was migrated to a unified `Media.set_id3_tags` method across all exports, and the transparent video background for title slides was solved with `nullsrc + format=rgba + geq` when a real background wasn’t available.

We also planned the physical distribution layer: custom SD cards with printed covers for the local Meron community, versioned as “March 2026 Edition,” with enhanced Suno‑backed editions to follow once the music stems were ready.  The session turned a scattered collection of cloned verses into a complete, polished, mathematically balanced audio library ready for grassroots distribution.
