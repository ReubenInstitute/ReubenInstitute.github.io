# 11 Jan 2026 – DeepSeek 3 – Aramaic noun series and production enhancements

_Aramaic_

We pivoted the Aramaic course strategy to create an accessible, public‑facing content funnel while continuing to refine the advanced subscriber curriculum.

### Class 4: Pronouns draft

We evaluated the existing draft for Class 4, analysing the pedagogical structure of its dialogue tables and refining the visual specifications for the Jasper Morrison‑inspired wooden figurines.  Each character — Avraham, Miryam, Binyamin, Rivka — now has a defined neutral base with specific attributes.

### The strategic pivot to a noun series

Recognising an opportunity for broader engagement, we conceived a new series of short, public educational videos teaching Aramaic nouns (starting with animals) by letter.  The format is simple and direct: a letter slide with its IPA sound, followed by video clips of objects labelled with their Aramaic words.  The implementation in `animals.json` and `course.py` was examined in detail, including safe zones for text overlays and a flexible positioning system.

### Production quality

A powerful non‑verbal cue was designed: a wooden arm points directly at the viewer to create a “your turn” moment, inviting participation without instruction.  For audio, an FFmpeg filter chain was specified — `loudnorm` for consistent volume, compressor, equaliser, and subtle reverb — to normalise and enhance the AI‑cloned narration across all videos.  An optimised TikTok description was crafted to launch the series effectively.

The session shifted from refining an advanced lesson for subscribers to architecting a complete, high‑quality content funnel that attracts a wider audience to the Institute’s mission.
