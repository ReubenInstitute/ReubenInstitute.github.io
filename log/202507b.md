# Jul 2025 – Gemini 2.5 – Brand identity, moderation, and Shabbat observance

_Brand, Community_

The channel’s persona is scholarly, authoritative, and professional, sustained by a high‑quality pre‑recorded voice and consistent use of plural pronouns (“we”, “our team”).  The main video content remains serious, but replies, comments, and slideshow‑style videos adopt a looser, funnier tone that suits TikTok’s culture.

Moderation follows unwritten rules of respect and good conduct, not a published list.  Only “poisonous” content is removed – no over‑censorship.

A universal two‑strike system applies:
* First offence: the off‑topic, disrespectful, or malicious comment is silently deleted.  The deletion itself is the signal.
* Second offence: the same user is blocked without warning.  At that point intent is clearly malicious, and blocking protects the community.  This includes followers.

The channel observes Shabbat by abstaining from all public engagement – posting, liking, commenting.  The sole exception is deletion of a genuinely malicious comment, which is a necessary act to prevent greater harm.  The time is used to thoughtfully prepare replies and upcoming content.

The community is largely self‑regulating.  The vast majority of poisonous comments come from external, non‑following users, confirming that the strict moderation shields the loyal audience from trolls rather than alienating it.
