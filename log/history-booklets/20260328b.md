# 28 Mar 2026 – DeepSeek 3, Gemini 3 – Lag BaOmer workflow validation, Parts 1–2 written

_HistoryBooklets_

The Pesach booklet proved the workflow could produce a complete draft in a single session, but that was a biblical holiday with a rich source base.  Lag BaOmer was the real test: a post‑biblical, minor festival with thinner primary material and a more diffuse identity.  If the workflow worked here, it would work anywhere.

We applied the same method—define the five‑part structure, gather references, convert selected sources into block‑quote format, write connective narrative, polish with Gemini—and it held.  Part 1 (Foundations) was completed with a reference list that anchored the holiday in its historical and Talmudic context.  Part 2 (Evolution) traced the medieval emergence, the Zohar’s transformative role, the Safed revolution, halakhic codification, and the Hasidic embrace.

After Gemini polish, both parts were integrated into the master file.  Duplicate sections and leftover bullet lists were stripped, leaving a clean document with Parts 1 and 2 fully written and placeholders for Parts 3, 4, 5, and Tales.

The workflow was validated as robust and transferable.  Minimal prompts, no back‑and‑forth churn, and the heavy lifting done by systematically feeding curated references, then layering narrative and polish in two clean passes.  Next: Part 3, Practice.
