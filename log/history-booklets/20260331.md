# 31 Mar 2026 – DeepSeek 3, Gemini 3 – Blockquote formatting disaster, LLM limits confirmed

_HistoryBooklets, Documentation_

What should have been a straightforward formatting pass became the most expensive cautionary tale in the project’s history.

The Pesach booklet existed in both English and Hebrew, and the blockquotes needed to conform to a strict house style: single `>`, underscore italics, colon‑introduced speech on a new line with `&emsp;`, bold straight double quotes, nested single quotes, Hebrew punctuation matched exactly to English.  The rules were laid out upfront in precise detail.  Two models were deployed.  Neither could execute reliably.

Across roughly 14 hours, the session generated 118 user prompts, 47 explicit rejections (a 39.8% failure rate), 27 all‑caps prompts, and 189 uses of the word “fuck.”  The worst loops hit 12 iterations for a single Zohar quote, 11 for the Song of the Sea, 9 for Talmud Sotah, and 8 for Midrash Tanchuma.

The final files were eventually polished to the required format, but the cost—in time, frustration, and trust—was indefensible.

The root cause was a fundamental category error: language models are probabilistic, rule‑interpreting engines, not deterministic executors.  No amount of upfront rule‑setting can make them reliably apply complex formatting rules across hundreds of discrete items.  The task belonged to a deterministic script.

This session now serves as the project’s definitive negative benchmark.  It is cited internally as evidence that LLMs must never be used for strict, rule‑based text formatting at scale.  The lesson is preserved in the log as a permanent reminder.
