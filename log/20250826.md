The final piece of the data‑layer puzzle was integrating the Bible and its applications—Psalms, Articles, Parashot—into the unified platform.  The session’s key achievement was designing the “Master Object” architecture for `Bible.py`, and it went exactly as it should: smoothly.

### The Master Object decision

We chose to keep all the Bible logic in a single file, making the `Bible` class a central hub that orchestrates its own applications internally.  Access is clean and intuitive:

```python
bible.psalms[22]      # Psalm 23
bible.articles[0]     # First article
bible.parashot[0]     # Bereshit portion
```

No external orchestration, no scattered imports.  The Bible knows its own applications, and the entire platform can reach them through a single entry point.

### Implementation

We implemented the architecture, polished `Bible.py` to the established standard, and integrated it into `Scriptures.py`.  The result: 100% of the platform’s data layer was now complete, consistent, and accessible from one intuitive API.
