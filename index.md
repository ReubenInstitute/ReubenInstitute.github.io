# Reuben Institute

![LOGO](/@@/logo.png)

_**"obsession for accuracy"**_

Reuben Institute is a scholarly organization and publishing studio dedicated to disseminating
and publishing foundational Hebrew and Aramaic texts. Motivated by the prevalence of inaccuracies
in existing published editions, the Institute utilises ancient, public‑domain source materials to
produce meticulously curated content across **print**, **digital**, **audio**, and **video** formats.
By combining rigorous academic standards with modern technology, the Institute ensures that
historical traditions remain accessible and accurate for contemporary audiences.

## Multimedia Series

### 🖼 [Daily Psalm](media/PsalmsSeries.md)

A psalm a day, verse by verse, accompanied by the Institute's serene fog‑and‑voice aesthetic and the now‑iconic baritone cloned from a 1976 recording. It's less a video series and more a quiet, daily moment of immersion — ancient words, simply presented.

### 🖼 [Weekly Parashah](media/ParashahSeries.md)

The Torah portion of the week, read in full with the same meticulous Hebrew narration. Conceived as a steady, listener‑friendly companion to the annual synagogue cycle, it serves both those who want to hear the text read beautifully and those who study it deeply.

### 🖼 [Piyutim](media/Piyutim.md)

Liturgical poetry and sacred song re‑imagined through AI‑generated multi‑track compositions and visual stem renderings. From timeless traditional melodies to original arrangements, the series treats the piyut as a living, breathing art form.

### 🖼 [Aramaic Class](media/AramaicClass.md)

A structured, hands‑on introduction to the language of the Talmud and Zohar, built around figurine‑based visual teaching and authentic Eastern pronunciation. It starts with the simplest nouns and patiently walks the learner all the way through to real reading competence.

## Android Apps

### 📱 [HebrewDate](apps/HebrewDateAndroidApp.md)

An Android widget that displays the Hebrew date alongside the civil date. No ads. Runs offline. That's it.

### 📱 [Psalms](apps/PsalmsAndroidApp.md)

The complete Book of Psalms in Hebrew, with the Institute's text and the same cloned voice used in the Daily Psalm series. Suited to reading a single verse or working through the whole book.

### 📱 [Scriptures](apps/ScripturesAndroidApp.md)

Torah, Prophets, Writings, and the core texts of the Oral Law in a single Android app. The Institute's full text corpus, navigable, with attention to RTL typography.

## [IVR](media/IVR.md)

A phone‑based interface to the Institute's audio library, for those without smartphones or who prefer no screen. Keypress menus give access to the daily Psalm, the weekly Parashah, and a voice‑guided quiz.

## Booklets

### 📖 [Halakhah, Haggadah, and History](HalakhahHaggadahHistory.md)

A growing library of booklets that weaves legal rulings, narrative legends, and historical context into a single, flowing read. Each volume takes one holiday or practice — Pesach, Hanukkah, Lag BaOmer — and traces its journey from biblical roots through rabbinic discussion and into living custom, always anchored in primary sources.

### 📖 Weekly Parashah

A clean, print‑ready companion for each week's Torah portion, designed to sit alongside the Institute's Weekly Parashah video series. It gathers the Hebrew text, a faithful translation, and a curated selection of commentary, all in a format meant for the Shabbat table or a quiet study session. ([Crowdfunding Campaign](CampaignParashah.md) is underway to bring each portion to print.)

### 📖 Zaggin diDhava (Bells of Gold)

A small, jewel‑like volume whose Aramaic title promises something precious. Without giving away its contents, it's the kind of booklet the Institute does best: a tightly focused exploration where language, source‑text, and design meet — part anthology, part commentary, and entirely a delight for the attentive reader.

## Products

### [Hebrew Clock](HebrewClock.md)

A timepiece that tells the hour the ancient way — dividing daylight and darkness into twelve proportional parts, shifting with the seasons. More a conceptual instrument than a standard clock, it connects modern timekeeping to the temporal rhythm of Hebrew prayer and tradition. It's at once a practical tool, a curiosity, and a quiet reminder that time itself can be measured by a sacred calendar.

### [Meron Judaica](MeronJudaica.md)

A collaborative side-project born from a partnership with artisans in Meron, offering physical ritual items and small editions that carry a sense of place and tradition. It's kept deliberately separate from the Institute's core scholarly work.

## Libraries

They cover Hebrew text parsing, calendar and astronomical calculations, geometry, and even sensor‑data handling — the quiet, reusable building blocks that power the apps, the clock, and the production studio. Useful on their own, but best understood as the Institute's technical infrastructure with the lid off.
